# Outreach Playbook

## Outreach Objective

The outreach objective is to find qualified stranded gas, flare gas, marginal field, modular power, and related infrastructure opportunities that can be screened through the Stranded Energy Development Screen MVP.

The first outreach motion should validate three things:

- Who has real assets, data, capital, or execution capability.
- Which counterparties have urgent stranded energy problems.
- Which opportunities are credible enough to enter the client intake workflow.

## Ideal First 100 Contacts Mix

- 30 independent operators / gas producers
- 15 EPC or modular infrastructure vendors
- 15 investors / family offices
- 10 data center or industrial power users
- 10 midstream / gas processing contacts
- 10 legal, permitting, and environmental advisors
- 10 Nigeria/Africa energy contacts

## Qualification Questions

- Do you currently own, operate, finance, or advise on stranded gas, flare gas, marginal field, or modular power opportunities?
- Is there an active asset, site, or counterparty need behind the discussion?
- Is gas volume known or supported by production history?
- Is gas quality known, especially H2S, CO2, N2, and heating value?
- Is there a plausible monetization pathway such as power, pipeline tie-in, processing, industrial offtake, or data center power?
- Who controls the gas, land, permits, and commercial decision?
- Is the counterparty willing to share data under NDA?
- What decision or transaction does the screening output need to support?
- What timeline is driving the opportunity?

## Follow-Up Cadence

- Day 0: Send concise first outreach.
- Day 3-4: Send a short follow-up with one specific question.
- Day 7-10: Send a final helpful follow-up or ask for the right contact.
- After a response: schedule discovery within one week.
- After discovery: send the client intake form or NDA request within 24 hours.
- After data receipt: confirm whether the opportunity qualifies for a paid development screen.

## What Counts As A Qualified Opportunity

A qualified opportunity usually has:

- A named asset, site, owner, operator, or sponsor.
- A credible resource, such as known gas volume or production history.
- A plausible monetization pathway.
- A reachable decision-maker.
- Willingness to provide data under NDA if needed.
- Enough urgency to justify a screening memo or development concept.
- No obvious blocker that makes the project impossible before screening.

## When To Ask For NDA

Ask for an NDA when the counterparty is ready to share:

- Production data.
- Gas analysis.
- Facility drawings.
- Contracts.
- Maps or coordinates.
- Permits.
- Commercial terms.
- Counterparty-sensitive project information.

Do not let NDA process block basic qualification. Use public or high-level information first, then move to NDA when real project data is needed.

## When To Send The Client Intake Form

Send `docs/client-intake-form.md` after the counterparty confirms there is a real opportunity or repeat opportunity source. The intake form is appropriate when:

- A discovery call confirms a credible asset or need.
- The counterparty can identify who owns or controls the asset.
- The counterparty is willing to provide enough data for screening.
- There is a potential development, advisory, investment, or partnership path.

## When To Propose A Paid Development Screen

Propose a paid development screen when:

- The counterparty has a real asset or mandate.
- Minimum data is available or obtainable.
- The problem is material enough to justify structured analysis.
- The counterparty needs a memo, recommendation, or next-step plan.
- The screening output can support a commercial decision, partner discussion, investment committee, or development roadmap.

The paid screen should be positioned as a preliminary development product, not a bankable feasibility study or final engineering package.
