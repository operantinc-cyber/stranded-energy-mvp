# Messaging Guide

## A. Positioning Hierarchy

- Company category: AI-native energy infrastructure developer.
- Vision: We are building an AI-native energy infrastructure developer focused on stranded and underused energy assets.
- First wedge: Stranded gas, flare gas, marginal field redevelopment, and modular power infrastructure.
- Current MVP: A concierge development screen and internal development desk. It is the first proof point, not the final product.
- Long-term model: Development fees, success fees, project equity, carried interest, project partnerships, and operating intelligence.

## C. Language To Avoid

- SaaS.
- AI platform.
- Generic AI transformation language.
- Software tool as the main positioning.
- Dashboard as the company category.
- Platform, unless clearly qualified and not used as the main category.
- Users as the main audience.
- Productivity app framing.
- Generic workflow automation framing.

## D. How To Describe The MVP

The MVP is a concierge development screen. It is an internal development desk used to review opportunity data, size a concept, assess risks, compare monetization pathways, and package the next development actions.

It is the first proof point for the larger company, not the final product.

## E. How To Describe The Final Company

The final company is an AI-native energy infrastructure developer that uses autonomous agents and structured workflows to originate, screen, structure, and advance overlooked energy opportunities.

The company should sound like a development partner and project originator with internal leverage from agents, not a generic software vendor.

## F. One-Liners

- Public website one-liner: Building an AI-native energy developer for stranded assets.
- Investor one-liner: We are building an AI-native energy infrastructure developer that uses agentic workflows to originate and advance overlooked stranded asset opportunities.
- Operator one-liner: We help operators turn stranded gas, flare gas, and marginal assets into practical development concepts, risk views, and next-step memos.
- EPC/vendor one-liner: We package early modular energy opportunities and identify where EPC and vendor input can turn a concept into a practical project path.
- Offtaker one-liner: We screen dedicated power and modular energy supply options for data center and industrial loads that need credible early-stage site and supply concepts.

AI-native energy infrastructure developer focused on stranded gas, flare gas, marginal fields, and modular power, starting with a concierge development screen and growing into autonomous project origination.

### 100-word version

We are building an AI-native energy infrastructure developer focused on stranded and underused energy assets. The first wedge is stranded gas, flare gas, marginal field redevelopment, and modular power infrastructure. The current concierge development screen is the first proof point: an internal development desk that uses autonomous agents and structured workflows to review intake data, screen opportunities, size concepts, assess risk, compare monetization pathways, and package a decision-ready memo.
