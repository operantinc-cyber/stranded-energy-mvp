Speaker notes:

This deck introduces the business thesis for an AI-native energy infrastructure developer. The goal is to move credible projects faster from raw opportunity to a decision-ready development concept through autonomous project origination and structured development workflows.

## Slide 2: The Problem

Bullets:

- Stranded gas and flaring remain common across operating regions.
- Marginal fields often have resource value but weak development packaging.
- Feasibility screening is slow, expensive, and fragmented.
- Technical, commercial, regulatory, and infrastructure data usually sit in separate conversations.
- Many early-stage projects die before they become financeable.

Speaker notes:

The issue is not only that energy is stranded. The issue is that early project information is scattered, incomplete, and hard to turn into a practical concept. Smaller or messier opportunities often never receive enough structured development work to prove whether they are worth pursuing.

## Slide 3: Why Now

Bullets:

- Power demand is rising across industrial, grid, and digital infrastructure markets.
- Data center and industrial load growth is increasing interest in dedicated power.
- Modular energy infrastructure can move faster than large conventional projects.
- Operators are looking for monetization pathways for flared, discounted, or shut-in gas.
- AI can compress early-stage screening and project packaging.

Speaker notes:

The timing is driven by real demand and real constraints. Loads need power, operators need monetization, and modular infrastructure is increasingly credible. AI is useful here when it is tied to a disciplined workflow, not as a substitute for engineering or commercial judgment.

## Slide 4: The Opportunity

Bullets:

- Stranded and underused energy assets can become modular power, EPF, industrial energy, flare monetization, or redevelopment projects.
- Many opportunities are too small, local, or messy for traditional developers.
- A faster screening/development workflow can unlock overlooked projects.
- Repeatable screening creates a pipeline of development candidates.

Speaker notes:

The opportunity is to focus on projects that are economically meaningful but not naturally served by large development teams. A repeatable screen can identify which ones deserve deeper work and which ones should be rejected quickly.

## Slide 5: Our Wedge

Bullets:

- Stranded gas-to-power.
- Flare gas monetization.
- Marginal field modular infrastructure.
- Gas-to-industrial or data center power.
- Initial focus on practical, modular, screenable opportunities.

Speaker notes:

The wedge is intentionally narrow. We start with opportunities that can be described with available data, screened with practical assumptions, and translated into a development memo. The early product is not trying to solve every energy development problem.

The advantage compounds through repetition. Each screened opportunity improves the method, the templates, the counterparty map, and the opportunity database. The long-term asset is not a generic AI interface; it is a development system with market-specific knowledge and relationships.
