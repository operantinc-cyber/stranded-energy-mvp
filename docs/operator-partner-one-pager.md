# Operator Partner One-Pager: Stranded Gas and Marginal Asset Monetization

Audience: Independent oil and gas operators, gas producers, marginal field owners.

## The Operator Problem

Operators often have gas that is stranded, flared, shut in, discounted, or difficult to monetize through existing infrastructure. Marginal assets may also need modular redevelopment concepts before they can attract partners, capital, or offtake.

## What We Evaluate

- Stranded gas.
- Flare gas.
- Shut-in or discounted gas.
- Marginal field redevelopment.
- Modular power.
- Modular EPF concepts.
- Gas-to-industrial power.
- Data center dedicated power.
- Pipeline tie-in or gas processing alternatives.

## What The Operator Receives

- Intake and data gap review.
- Resource and infrastructure screen.
- Gas-to-power or modular infrastructure sizing.
- Preliminary economics.
- Risk register.
- Monetization pathway comparison.
- Recommended development concept.
- Go/no-go recommendation.
- Practical next actions.
- Exportable development memo.

## Typical Asset Fit

- Known gas volume or production history.
- Flaring, shut-in, stranded, or discounted gas.
- Existing or potential site access.
- Potential power, industrial, pipeline, processing, or redevelopment pathway.
- Decision-maker willing to share data under NDA.

## Required Input Data

- Basic asset description.
- Gas volume and production history.
- Gas analysis if available.
- Infrastructure distances and site access.
- Land, gas, and mineral rights context.
- Existing contracts or offtake constraints.
- Permitting and environmental context.
- Available drawings, maps, photos, or studies.

## Engagement Options

- Quick opportunity diagnostic.
- Full development screen.
- Advanced pre-feasibility support.
- Development partnership.

## Why Partner Early

Early screening helps operators avoid wasting time on weak concepts and identify the strongest monetization path before spending on engineering, legal work, or vendor quotes. It also creates a concise memo that can support internal approvals, partner discussions, or investor conversations.

## Next Step

Start with the client intake form and a short discovery call. If the opportunity is credible, we can prepare a preliminary development screen and recommend the next practical action.
