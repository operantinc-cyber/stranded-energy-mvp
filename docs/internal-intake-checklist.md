# Internal Intake Checklist

Use this checklist before creating or screening a new opportunity in the MVP. The goal is to decide whether the data is sufficient for a preliminary screen, what follow-up is needed, and whether the opportunity should proceed, pause, or be rejected.

## Minimum Viable Data Required To Screen

- Named asset or opportunity.
- Asset type and current operating status.
- Country, state, region, or basin.
- Operator, owner, or credible counterparty contact.
- Approximate available gas volume in MMscfd.
- Flaring, shut-in, or current gas use status.
- Basic gas quality information or confirmation that gas analysis is unavailable.
- Approximate distance to pipeline, power line, road, or load.
- Land availability or land control status.
- Any known offtake pathway or commercial objective.
- Known permitting, environmental, community, or security constraints.
- Decision-maker and timeline for next steps.

## Data Quality Questions

- Is the gas volume measured, estimated, or speculative?
- Is production stable enough to support continuous power generation?
- Is gas composition recent and from the relevant source?
- Are H2S, CO2, and N2 values known?
- Are infrastructure distances verified or only estimated?
- Is land available for equipment, access, and utilities?
- Is there a credible buyer or internal use case for power or gas?
- Are the owner, operator, mineral rights holder, and landowner aligned?
- Are permits already in place or likely to be required?
- Is the counterparty willing to provide documents under NDA?

## Red Flags

- Unclear gas ownership.
- Unclear land control.
- No realistic offtake.
- Severe H2S/CO2 with no technical partner.
- No road or site access.
- Security issues that limit field execution.
- Unclear permits or known permitting blockage.
- Unrealistic timeline.
- Speculative resource with no production history.
- Counterparty unwilling to sign NDA or provide data.
- Disputed asset title or operator authority.
- No identifiable decision-maker.
- Power price or gas value expectations far outside local market reality.

## Go / No-Go Pre-Screen Rules

- Proceed if gas volume, site access, land, and offtake path are directionally credible.
- Proceed with caution if gas quality, permits, or commercial control are incomplete but follow-up data is obtainable.
- Pause if ownership, land control, or offtake is unclear and no decision-maker can resolve it.
- Reject if the resource is speculative, inaccessible, legally disputed, or commercially unsupported.
- Reject if the timeline requires commitments before technical, legal, or commercial diligence can be performed.

## Missing Data Follow-Up Questions

- Can you provide the most recent production history?
- Can you provide a gas analysis with H2S, CO2, N2, methane, and heating value?
- Who owns the gas, land, and mineral rights?
- Are there existing gas sales, processing, power, or gathering contracts?
- Are there any active permits or prior regulatory filings?
- What is the nearest viable power offtake, industrial load, pipeline, or substation?
- Is land available for modular power, processing, compression, or storage equipment?
- Are there known community, environmental, or security concerns?
- Who will approve the next phase of diligence?
- What timeline is driving the opportunity?

## When To Bring In An Advisor

- Engineering advisor: gas composition is complex, H2S/CO2 is elevated, equipment sizing is uncertain, or site layout is constrained.
- Legal advisor: gas ownership, land rights, mineral rights, contracts, or permitting authority are unclear.
- Environmental advisor: air permits, emissions, water, waste, community issues, or environmental legacy liabilities are present.
- Interconnection advisor: grid export, substation tie-in, power line upgrade, or utility approval is needed.
- Financial advisor: the opportunity requires debt, tax structuring, project finance, contracted revenue, or investment committee materials.
- Security advisor: field access, personnel safety, or country risk is material.

## When To Reject Or Pause An Opportunity

- Reject when the counterparty cannot demonstrate asset control or authority.
- Reject when no practical monetization pathway exists.
- Reject when site access, land, or safety issues make execution unrealistic.
- Pause when core technical data is missing but obtainable.
- Pause when an NDA or data sharing approval is pending.
- Pause when the decision-maker is not identified.
- Pause when legal, permitting, or interconnection questions control feasibility.
